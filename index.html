WHAT IS FIREDANCING?

Ancient Aztecs performed a fire dance dedicated to Xiuhtecuhtli, the god of fire. The Aztec fire dance is performed today for tourists in Mexico. In Bali, the Angel Dance and the Fire Dance, regularly performed for tourists, have origins in ancient rituals. Both the Angel Dance and the Fire Dance originated in a trance ritual called the sanghyang, a ritual dance “performed to ward off witches at the time of an epidemic.” Also known as the “horse dance” men perform the dance by holding rods representing horses, while leaping around burning coconut husks, and walking through the flames. French Polynesia, Antigua, Cuba and Saint Lucia are other locations where fire dances are recreated for tourists. The Siddha Jats of the Thar Desert in India perform traditional fire dances as part of the Spring festival. Fire dancing is performed to music played on drums and the behr. There are variations of the fire dancing; men often perform a dance that involves walking on hot coals. A large fire is created and allowed to burn down until it is a pit of glowing embers. The performers then jump in and out of the pit kicking up the embers to create showers of sparks while women perform a dance while balancing flaming tin pots on their heads. Today this ritual is often performed for tourists.

ABOUT US

Fire Rhythm was founded in Pretoria in 2003. Its founding members were four cousins who practised and performed together under the name Fire Kidz. These cousins worked together to create their own unique routines, which they performed at weddings, corporate functions and other gatherings. They were largely successful and quickly became a popular act in Pretoria, garnering plenty of support and positive testimonials. However, as the years wore on and its eldest member turned twenty, the moniker "Fire Kidz" was no longer quite so accurate. Fire Rhythm rose from the ashes of Fire Kidz, maintaining their popularity, professionalism and, most importantly, their techincal skill.
